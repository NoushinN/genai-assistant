Generative AI as an Assistant

4 Illustrative Examples of AI‑Assisted Work

This chapter presents illustrative examples of how generative AI can be used in research and investigative contexts. The examples are intentionally generic and are meant to demonstrate patterns of use rather than prescribe workflows.

4.1 Early‑stage inquiry

In the early stages of a project, AI systems can help broaden the space of possible questions or angles. For example, a researcher might ask for alternative ways of framing a problem or for related issues that could warrant attention. These outputs function as prompts for further thinking, not as research findings.

4.2 Drafting and revising analytical text

Generative AI can assist with revising drafts for clarity, organization, and tone. It may help identify ambiguities, suggest re‑ordering of material, or offer alternative phrasings. Substantive claims, interpretations, and evidentiary judgments must remain under human control.

4.3 Working across multiple sources

When dealing with large bodies of text, AI systems can help summarize themes or contrasts across documents. These summaries should be treated as navigational aids that support closer reading rather than substitutes for it.

Across these examples, the consistent theme is interpretive control: the usefulness of AI outputs depends on how they are evaluated and integrated by the researcher.
